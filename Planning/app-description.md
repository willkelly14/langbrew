# LangBrew — Complete App Description

## What is LangBrew?

LangBrew is a language learning app built around one core idea: **you learn a language by understanding it, not by memorizing it.** Instead of flashcard drills and grammar exercises in isolation, LangBrew immerses you in real reading, AI-powered conversation, and smart review — all matched to your level and interests.

Most language apps treat learning like a game — quick dopamine hits from matching words, filling blanks, and earning points. But that approach rarely leads to real fluency. LangBrew takes a different path, grounded in decades of research on language acquisition. When you read something you genuinely understand, when you have a conversation about something you actually care about, your brain doesn't just memorize — it acquires the language naturally.

## The Philosophy

The name "langbrew" reflects the idea that fluency is something you brew over time — slowly, naturally, through daily habit. Like a good cup of coffee, it can't be rushed. You pour in a little effort each day, and over time something rich and lasting develops. The app's hand-drawn coffee mug logo and warm linen aesthetic reinforce this feeling — calm, unhurried, inviting. LangBrew never makes you feel tested or judged. It frames everything as natural progress through understanding.

## How It Works

LangBrew revolves around three connected pillars:

1. **Read at your level.** The app serves you real passages — stories, articles, excerpts — tuned to sit just beyond your current ability. Not so easy that you coast, not so hard that you stall. Vocabulary you don't know is highlighted in context, and tapping a word instantly shows its meaning without pulling you out of the reading flow. Over time, the difficulty adjusts as you grow. 

2. **Talk about what interests you.** AI-powered conversation partners let you practice speaking and writing in natural, open-ended dialogue. You pick a topic — travel, food, weekend plans, anything — and have a real conversation. The AI adapts to your level, gently corrects mistakes, and gives feedback afterward on grammar, vocabulary, and fluency.

3. **Review what's tricky.** Words and phrases you encounter while reading or talking are automatically collected into a personal Language Bank. From there, spaced repetition flashcards surface the ones you're most likely to forget, right when you need to see them again. The system is based on the forgetting curve — it knows when your memory is about to fade, and it steps in at exactly the right moment. If you encounter a word in your reading that is already in your word bank but you forgot, you can click to highlight it again and reset its flashcard so that it is treated as a new word. 

## Who It's For

LangBrew is for anyone who wants to learn a language through meaningful content rather than rote exercises. Whether you're a beginner picking up your first words or an intermediate learner looking to push into fluency, the app adapts to where you are. It's especially well-suited for people who love reading, enjoy conversation, and prefer a calmer, more thoughtful approach to learning over gamified streaks and leaderboards — though LangBrew does track your streaks and progress, it does so quietly, as encouragement rather than pressure.

## Design & Feel

The app has a warm, organic aesthetic — a linen-toned palette of creams and soft browns, elegant serif typography for headlines, and clean sans-serif for interface text. The hand-drawn coffee mug logo with "lb" inside sets the tone from the first screen: this is a space for slow, enjoyable learning. Every interaction, from tapping a word to reviewing a flashcard, is designed to feel natural and uncluttered.

## Reading Experience

### Reader View (4a)
The main reading screen. A top navigation bar shows a back arrow, the passage/chapter title centered, and icon buttons for listening mode and text options.

The body displays the passage text in a comfortable serif font with generous line spacing. Vocabulary words the user hasn't learned yet are highlighted with a subtle cream background and dotted underline.

**Table of Contents (4a-toc):** A bottom sheet listing all chapters with chapter numbers, titles, word counts, and reading progress percentages. The current chapter is highlighted.

### Word Tap (4b)
When the user taps a highlighted word, a bottom sheet popup appears showing:
- The word in large serif text
- Phonetic pronunciation in gray italic
- Word type badge (noun, verb, adjective, etc.)
- A speaker button to hear pronunciation
- The definition
- An example sentence in italic
- An "Add to Language Bank" button (auto-adds the word)

**Undo State (4b-undo):** After a word is added, the popup briefly shows an undo option in case the user didn't mean to save it.

### Word Long Press (4c)
Long-pressing a word reveals an expanded view with more detail — multiple definitions, conjugation hints, and usage context.

### Phrase Selection (4c2)
Users can drag to select a phrase (multiple words). The selection highlights the phrase and shows a translation popup for the full expression rather than individual words.

### Listening Mode (4d)
Toggles the passage into audio playback mode. The text dims except for the currently spoken sentence, which is highlighted and tracked as the audio plays. Controls at the bottom include:
- Rewind 30 seconds
- Play/pause button (large, centered)
- Forward 30 seconds
- Speed control (0.5x to 2.0x)
- A progress bar showing playback position

### Text Options (4e)
A bottom sheet for customizing the reading experience:
- **Font Size:** A slider from small to large with a live size preview
- **Line Spacing:** Three options — Compact, Normal, Relaxed
- **Font:** Serif or Sans-serif toggle
- **Theme:** Light, Sepia, or Dark

### Voice Options (4f)
A bottom sheet for choosing the narration voice. Six voice options are listed, each with:
- An avatar illustration
- A name (Mia, Carlos, Elena, Lucía, Diego, Marco)
- A personality tag (e.g., "Friendly · Natural", "Calm · Clear", "Expressive · Warm")
- A preview speaker button to sample the voice
- A checkmark on the selected voice

Below the voice list, a **Speed slider** lets the user adjust playback speed from 0.5x to 2.0x, with the current value displayed (default: 1.0x).

A "Save Option" button confirms the selection.

## Design Language

**Palette:** Warm Linen — cream backgrounds (#f3f0ea), deep brown-black text (#2a2318), soft grays for secondary elements, and a subtle highlight yellow (#ede8d2) for vocabulary words.

**Typography:** Instrument Serif for headlines and literary elements; Instrument Sans for UI text and labels. The combination feels warm and bookish without being stuffy.

**Components:** Rounded cards (12px radius) with subtle shadows, pill-shaped tags and filters, bottom sheets for contextual actions, and a persistent tab bar for main navigation.

**Tone:** Encouraging, calm, and non-intimidating. The app never makes the user feel tested — it frames everything as natural progress through understanding.
